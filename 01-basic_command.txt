What is in here? (ls)
Command: ls (List)
What it does: Lists all files and directories in your current location.
Useful flags:
ls -l: Shows "long" format (permissions, file size, owner, and modification date).
ls :-> list all the files and folders included in current path
example:-
    ~ $ ls
    chetan  chetana  demo.txt  devops  devops1  testing.txt
    ~ $ 
ls -a: Shows "all" files, including hidden ones (files that start with a .).
ls -lart  :-> list all the details with hidden files and folders.
example:-
    ~ $ ls -lart

How do I move? (cd)
Command: cd (Change Directory)
What it does: Moves you from one folder to another.
Common patterns:
cd Documents: Moves you into the Documents folder.
cd ..: Moves you up one level to the parent directory.
        devops $ pwd
        /home/cloudshell-user/devops
        devops $ ls -l
        total 8
        -rw-r--r--. 1 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user   64 May  9 11:51 chetan.txt
        drwxr-xr-x. 2 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 May  9 13:20 subdevops
        -rw-r--r--. 1 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user    0 May  9 13:16 usertest_1.txt
        -rw-r--r--. 1 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user    0 May  9 13:16 usertest_2.txt
        -rw-r--r--. 1 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user    0 May  9 13:16 usertest_3.txt
        devops $ cd ..
        ~ $ ls -l
        total 16
        drwxrwxrwx. 3 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 May  9 13:17 chetan
        dr-x------. 2 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 May  9 13:08 chetana
        -rw-r--r--. 1 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user    0 May  9 12:47 demo.txt
        drwxr-xr-x. 3 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 May  9 13:20 devops
        dr--------. 2 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 May  9 12:24 devops1
        -rw-r--r--. 1 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user    0 May  9 13:09 testing.txt

cd ~: Returns you immediately to your Home directory.
        ~ $ pwd
        /home/cloudshell-user
        ~ $ cd devops
        devops $ cd subdevops/
        subdevops $ pwd
        /home/cloudshell-user/devops/subdevops
        subdevops $ cd ~
        ~ $ ped
        -bash: ped: command not found
        ~ $ pwd
        /home/cloudshell-user

cd -: Returns you to the previous directory you were just in. 
        ~ $ pwd
        /home/cloudshell-user
        ~ $ cd devops/subdevops/
        subdevops $ pwd
        /home/cloudshell-user/devops/subdevops

Making and Removing
mkdir [name] (Make Directory): Creates a new folder.
        ~ $ pwd
        /home/cloudshell-user
        ~ $ mkdir devops-practice
        ~ $ ls -l
        total 20
        drwxrwxrwx. 3 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 May  9 13:17 chetan
        dr-x------. 2 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 May  9 13:08 chetana
        -rw-r--r--. 1 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user    0 May  9 12:47 demo.txt
        drwxr-xr-x. 3 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 May  9 13:20 devops
        dr--------. 2 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 May  9 12:24 devops1
        drwxr-xr-x. 2 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 Jun 25 14:12 devops-practice
        -rw-r--r--. 1 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user    0 May  9 13:09 testing.txt
        ~ $        

touch [filename]: Creates an empty file.
        devops-practice $ touch test_file.sh
        devops-practice $ pwd
        /home/cloudshell-user/devops-practice
        devops-practice $ ls 
        test_file.sh
        devops-practice $
        devops-practice $ touch readme.txt
        devops-practice $ ls
        readme.txt  test_file.sh
        devops-practice $ 

rm [filename]: Deletes a file. Be careful: There is no "Trash Bin" in the terminal; once it's gone, it's gone.
        devops-practice $ pwd
        /home/cloudshell-user/devops-practice
        devops-practice $ ls
        readme.txt  test_file.sh
        devops-practice $ rm test_file.sh 
        devops-practice $ ls
        readme.txt
        devops-practice $ 

rmdir [name]: Removes an empty directory.
        devops-practice $ mkdir test_dir
        devops-practice $ ls -lart
        total 12
        drwxrwxrwx. 9 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 Jun 25 14:14 ..
        -rw-r--r--. 1 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user    0 Jun 25 14:17 readme.txt
        drwxr-xr-x. 2 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 Jun 25 14:19 test_dir
        drwxr-xr-x. 3 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 Jun 25 14:19 .
        devops-practice $ rmdir test_dir/
        devops-practice $ ls -lart
        total 8
        drwxrwxrwx. 9 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 Jun 25 14:14 ..
        -rw-r--r--. 1 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user    0 Jun 25 14:17 readme.txt
        drwxr-xr-x. 2 cloudshell-user cloudshell-user 4096 Jun 25 14:20 .
        devops-practice $ 

rm -r [name]: Removes a directory and everything inside it (recursive delete). Use this with extreme caution!
        ~ $ pwd
        /home/cloudshell-user
        ~ $ ls
        chetan  chetana  demo.txt  devops  devops1  devops-practice  test_file.sh  testing.txt
        ~ $ rm -r devops-practice
        ~ $ ls
        chetan  chetana  demo.txt  devops  devops1  test_file.sh  testing.txt
        ~ $ 

Viewing Content
cat [filename]: Concatenates and displays the entire content of a file on your screen. Best for small files.
        ~ $ ls
        chetan  chetana  demo.txt  devops  devops1  test_file.sh  testing.txt
        ~ $ cat test_file.sh 
        yum install tree -y 

        #this is testing file,I have added some text here.

        yum install nginx -y 

        sudo su adduser
        ~ $ 

less [filename]: Opens the file in a scrollable view. Use q to exit. Great for long files.
         $ less test_file.sh  

head / tail: Displays the first or last 10 lines of a file, respectively.
        $ tail -5 test_file.sh                                                                                                                                 
        We have a fantastic library filled with countless books.
        The playground is spacious, allowing us to play and stay active.
        The teachers are incredibly kind, helpful, and supportive.
        I learn new things every day and study joyfully with friends.
        My school truly feels like a second home
        ~ $ less -5 test_file.sh
